Bookcafe | Mobile App / UX·UI

Making Reading an even more enjoyable moment by including your friends. Improve your reading experience with enhanced features like tabs, comments, and collaborative reading options, fostering a more engaging and social interaction.

Problem Statement

Martie is a book lover who needs a modern style mobile reading app that allows her to share her thoughts and experiences while reading a book because she wants to socialize by expressing her emotions and finding out others opinions. How might we create a mobile reading app with social features allowing Martie to share her thoughts and emotions about a book, while also discovering and engaging with others perspectives?

Research

After the tests, we identified some usability issues and received feedback. These issues included privacy concerns, missing parts of steps in wireframes for a more enhanced user experience, and other obscure usability issues that were not apparent to us before. 

I crafted a foundation for user-centric design by developing detailed user personas, mapping out user journeys, creating prioritization matrices, and constructing empathy maps. This methodical approach allowed me to gain deep insights into the target audience's needs, preferences, and pain points, providing a strategic framework to inform design decisions throughout the project. The integration of user personas, journey maps, prioritization matrices, and empathy maps in the design process ensured a holistic and empathetic understanding of the users, laying the groundwork for a meaningful and tailored reading experience within the app.

Iterations

Through the prototype, I emphasized the implementation of user-friendly mechanisms for shared reading, interactive discussions, and emotional expression, ensuring a engaging platform where users can read together and share their thoughts and experiences about the book. The interactive prototype showcases the intuitive navigation and the overall immersive environment designed to enhance the communal aspect of reading within the app.
